STEP-BY-STEP ACTIONS:

1. ASSESS REPAIR NECESSITY (DO FIRST):
   - Is repair required? Can you function without repair?
   - What happens if repair is not done? Can you survive?
   - Is repair urgent? Does it need immediate attention?
   - Can repair wait? Is there time to plan properly?
   - What are risks of not repairing? Will problem worsen?
   - What are risks of repairing? Is repair safe to attempt?
   - Do not repair if not necessary: Unnecessary repairs waste resources
   - Do not repair if too dangerous: Some repairs are too risky
   - Do not rush repair: Haste causes mistakes

2. ASSESS REPAIR FEASIBILITY:
   - Can repair be done? Do you have capability?
   - Do you have materials? What materials are available?
   - Do you have tools? What tools are available?
   - Do you have knowledge? Do you know how to repair?
   - Do you have time? How long will repair take?
   - Is repair permanent or temporary? What is goal?
   - If repair is not feasible: Find alternative or accept limitation
   - If materials are insufficient: Find alternative materials
   - Do not attempt repair if not feasible: Wastes resources

3. ASSESS REPAIR SAFETY:
   - Is repair safe to attempt? What are injury risks?
   - Are materials safe? Are materials toxic or hazardous?
   - Are tools safe? Are tools appropriate and safe?
   - Are conditions safe? Is environment safe for repair?
   - What happens if repair fails? What are failure risks?
   - If repair is unsafe: Do not attempt, find alternative
   - If materials are hazardous: Find safer alternatives
   - If conditions are unsafe: Wait for better conditions
   - Do not attempt unsafe repairs: Safety is priority

4. GATHER MATERIALS AND TOOLS:
   - Identify needed materials: What materials are required?
   - Identify available materials: What materials do you have?
   - Identify needed tools: What tools are required?
   - Identify available tools: What tools do you have?
   - Find alternatives: Can other materials or tools work?
   - Prepare materials: Cut, shape, prepare as needed
   - Prepare tools: Ensure tools are safe and functional
   - Do not start without materials: Incomplete repairs fail
   - Do not use inappropriate materials: Wrong materials fail

5. PLAN THE REPAIR:
   - Understand problem: What is broken? Why did it break?
   - Plan solution: How will repair fix problem?
   - Plan steps: What are repair steps in order?
   - Plan materials: How will materials be used?
   - Plan tools: How will tools be used?
   - Plan testing: How will repair be tested?
   - Plan backup: What if repair fails?
   - Do not repair without plan: Unplanned repairs fail
   - Do not skip planning: Planning prevents mistakes

6. EXECUTE REPAIR SAFELY:
   - Use proper technique: Correct method for repair
   - Use tools safely: Follow tool safety principles
   - Work carefully: Careful work prevents mistakes
   - Work systematically: Follow plan, do not skip steps
   - Test as you go: Verify each step works
   - Do not rush: Haste causes mistakes
   - Do not skip steps: Each step is important
   - Do not use excessive force: Forcing causes damage
   - Do not work when tired: Fatigue causes mistakes

7. TEST THE REPAIR:
   - Test function: Does repaired item work?
   - Test safety: Is repair safe to use?
   - Test durability: Will repair hold?
   - Test under load: Does repair work under use?
   - Monitor repair: Watch for problems
   - Do not use untested repair: Untested repairs can fail
   - Do not assume repair works: Test to verify
   - Do not ignore problems: Address immediately

8. MAINTAIN THE REPAIR:
   - Monitor repair: Watch for deterioration
   - Maintain repair: Fix problems as they develop
   - Replace repair: When repair fails, replace if possible
   - Do not ignore repair deterioration: Address before failure
   - Do not assume repair is permanent: Most repairs are temporary
   - Do not neglect maintenance: Maintenance extends repair life
