TITLE: Food Poisoning and Unsafe Food Illness

WHEN:
- Vomiting
- Diarrhea
- Stomach cramps
- Nausea after eating

MAIN DANGERS:
- Dehydration
- Weakness
- Infection spread

IMMEDIATE STEPS:
1. Stop eating suspected food immediately
2. Rest in a cool, clean place
3. Drink clean water in small, frequent sips
4. Use ORS if available

IF VOMITING:
- Do not force food
- Take small sips of water every few minutes

DO NOT:
- Do not stop fluids
- Do not take unknown medicines
- Do not take anti-diarrheal drugs without guidance

SEEK HELP IF:
- Blood in vomit or stool
- Severe weakness or confusion
- High fever
- Symptoms last more than 3 days

PREVENTION:
- Cook food thoroughly
- Eat freshly cooked food
- Wash hands before handling food

SOURCE:
WHO Food Safety and Diarrheal Disease Guidelines
