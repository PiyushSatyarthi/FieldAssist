TITLE: Infection Prevention

KEYWORDS: infection prevention, hygiene, hand washing, sanitation, clean water, wound care

SEVERITY LEVELS:
- Prevention: Following proper hygiene practices
- Risk: Poor hygiene, contaminated materials
- Infection: Signs of infection present (see wound_infection_management.md)

WHEN TO USE THIS GUIDE:
- Before handling wounds or medical care
- Setting up medical care area
- General hygiene practices
- Preventing spread of infection
- Before and after medical procedures

IMMEDIATE PRIORITIES:
1. Prevent infection - Easier than treating
2. Maintain cleanliness - Critical for survival
3. Proper waste disposal - Prevents spread
4. Protect water sources - Prevents illness

IMMEDIATE DANGERS:
- Poor hygiene causes infections
- Infections can be fatal
- Contaminated water causes illness
- Waste contamination spreads disease
- Prevention is critical - infections are hard to treat without antibiotics

STEP-BY-STEP TREATMENT:

1. HAND WASHING:
   - Wash hands with soap and water
   - Scrub for at least 20 seconds
   - Wash before handling wounds
   - Wash after using toilet
   - Wash before eating
   - Use cleanest water available

2. CLEAN WATER:
   - Use clean water for medical care
   - Boil water if possible (1 minute rolling boil)
   - Filter water through clean cloth
   - Store water in clean containers
   - Protect water sources from contamination

3. WOUND CARE:
   - Clean wounds immediately
   - Use clean materials
   - Keep wounds covered
   - Change dressings regularly
   - See wound_care.md for complete care

4. WASTE DISPOSAL:
   - Dispose of waste properly
   - Keep waste away from water sources
   - Keep waste away from living areas
   - Bury waste or use latrines
   - Cover waste with soil

5. FOOD SAFETY:
   - Cook food thoroughly
   - Eat freshly cooked food
   - Wash hands before handling food
   - Store food properly
   - Avoid spoiled food

FOR SPECIFIC WOUND TYPES / SPECIAL CASES:

BEFORE MEDICAL PROCEDURES:
- Wash hands thoroughly
- Use cleanest materials available
- Clean area where procedure will occur
- Minimize contamination risk

AFTER MEDICAL PROCEDURES:
- Clean equipment if reusable
- Dispose of contaminated materials
- Wash hands
- Monitor for infection

WOUND CARE:
- Clean wounds immediately
- Use clean water and materials
- Keep wounds covered
- Change dressings regularly
- See wound_care.md

WATER PURIFICATION:
- Boil water if possible
- Filter through clean cloth
- Let sediment settle
- Use cleanest water available
- Protect water sources

WHAT YOU CAN DO:

- Keep medical area clean

- Dispose of waste properly
- Monitor for problems

MONITORING:

- Monitor water quality
- Check waste disposal
- Ensure practices are maintained

REALISTIC EXPECTATIONS:

WITH PROPER PREVENTION:
- Most infections can be prevented
- Proper hygiene reduces risk significantly
- Prevention is easier than treatment
- Most people can maintain basic hygiene

WITHOUT IDEAL CONDITIONS:
- Perfect cleanliness may not be possible
- Use cleanest available methods
- Some risk is unavoidable
- Do your best with available resources

DO NOT:
- DO NOT reuse dirty bandages (spreads infection)
- DO NOT ignore hygiene (causes infections)
- DO NOT contaminate water sources (causes illness)
- DO NOT leave waste near living areas (spreads disease)
- DO NOT ignore contamination (causes problems)
- DO NOT skip hand washing (critical for prevention)
