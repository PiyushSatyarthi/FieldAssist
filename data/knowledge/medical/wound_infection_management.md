TITLE: Managing Wound Infection Without Antibiotics

WHEN:
- Wound is red, warm, painful
- Mild pus present
- No access to antibiotics

GOAL:
- Prevent spread
- Reduce bacterial load
- Buy time

STEPS:
1. Wash hands thoroughly
2. Open wound gently if pus is trapped
3. Rinse with clean water
4. Allow wound to drain freely
5. Cover lightly with clean cloth
6. Clean at least once daily

IMPORTANT:
- Airflow helps healing
- Tight closure traps infection

DO NOT:
- Do not seal infected wounds
- Do not ignore worsening pain
- Do not reuse dirty bandages

DANGER SIGNS:
- Black tissue
- Rapid swelling
- Fever with confusion
- Red streaks spreading

OUTCOME:
- Some infections heal slowly
- Some will worsen even with care

SOURCE:
WHO Wound Care in Emergencies
