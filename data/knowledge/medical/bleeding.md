TITLE: Controlling Bleeding

WHEN:
- Blood flowing steadily
- Blood soaking cloth quickly

STEPS:
1. Ask the person to lie down if possible
2. Apply firm pressure directly on the wound using clean cloth
3. Elevate the injured part if possible
4. Maintain pressure for at least 10 minutes
5. If bleeding continues, apply another cloth and continue pressure

DO NOT:
- Do not remove objects stuck in the wound
- Do not release pressure too early

USE A TOURNIQUET ONLY IF:
- Bleeding is life-threatening
- Direct pressure fails

WARNING SIGNS:
- Bleeding does not stop
- Dizziness or fainting
- Pale or cold skin

SOURCE:
WHO Emergency First Aid Guidelines
