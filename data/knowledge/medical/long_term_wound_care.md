TITLE: Long-Term Wound Care (Days to Weeks)

WHEN:
- Open wound healing slowly
- No access to medical care
- No antibiotics available

GOAL:
- Prevent infection
- Allow natural healing
- Avoid tissue death

DAILY CARE STEPS:
1. Wash hands before touching wound
2. Rinse wound gently with clean water
3. Allow wound to drain if fluid is present
4. Cover lightly with clean cloth
5. Change dressing daily

IMPORTANT:
- Slight redness is normal
- Mild clear fluid is normal
- Airflow helps healing

DO NOT:
- Do not seal wounds tightly
- Do not ignore increasing pain
- Do not reuse dirty bandages

DANGER SIGNS:
- Black or dead tissue
- Strong bad smell
- Rapid swelling
- Fever with confusion

REALITY:
- Some wounds heal slowly
- Some wounds worsen even with care

SOURCE:
WHO Wound Care in Emergencies Manual
