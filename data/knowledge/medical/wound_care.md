TITLE: Cleaning and Dressing a Wound

WHEN:
- Cut, scrape, or open skin injury
- Bleeding has slowed or stopped

YOU WILL NEED:
- Clean water
- Clean cloth or bandage
- Soap (if available)
- Antiseptic (if available)

STEPS:
1. Wash your hands with soap and clean water
2. Rinse the wound gently with clean water to remove dirt
3. Do NOT scrub deep inside the wound
4. If available, apply antiseptic around (not deep inside) the wound
5. Cover with a clean cloth or bandage
6. Change dressing daily or when dirty or wet

DO NOT:
- Do not apply soil, ash, oil, or plant paste
- Do not tightly close deep or wide wounds
- Do not touch the wound with dirty hands

WARNING SIGNS:
- Increasing pain
- Redness spreading
- Swelling or pus
- Fever

SOURCE:
WHO First Aid Manual
