- Experiencing panic or severe anxiety
- Feeling overwhelmed by fear

IMMEDIATE PRIORITIES:
1. Recognize panic: Identify that you are panicking
2. Stop and breathe: Stop activity, focus on breathing
3. Assess situation: What is actual situation?
4. Use control techniques: Apply panic control methods
5. Restore thinking: Regain clear thinking
6. Make decisions: Make decisions based on clear thinking
7. Prevent recurrence: Take steps to prevent panic
8. Help others: Assist others if they are panicking

IMMEDIATE DANGERS:
- Poor decisions: Panic causes poor decision-making
- Physical impairment: Panic affects physical capability
- Resource waste: Panic wastes resources
- Time waste: Panic wastes time
- Injury: Panic can cause injury
- Group problems: Panic affects group coordination
- Communication failure: Panic impairs communication
- Exhaustion: Panic is physically exhausting
- Further panic: Panic can trigger more panic
- Death: Panic can lead to fatal mistakes

STEP-BY-STEP ACTIONS:

1. RECOGNIZE PANIC (DO FIRST):
   - Physical signs: Rapid heartbeat, rapid breathing, sweating, trembling
   - Mental signs: Confusion, inability to think, tunnel vision, racing thoughts
   - Emotional signs: Extreme fear, feeling overwhelmed, feeling out of control
   - Behavioral signs: Inability to act, frozen, or reckless action
   - If panicking: Recognize immediately
   - Do not deny panic: Denial prevents control
   - Do not ignore signs: Signs indicate panic

2. STOP AND BREATHE:
   - Stop activity: Stop whatever you are doing
   - Focus on breathing: Concentrate on breathing
   - Breathe slowly: Slow, deep breaths
   - Count breaths: Count to focus mind
   - Breathe deeply: Deep breaths calm body
   - Do not rush: Rushing increases panic
   - Do not ignore breathing: Breathing is foundation of control

3. ASSESS ACTUAL SITUATION:
   - What is real threat? Separate real from perceived
   - What is actual danger? Assess actual danger level
   - What can you do? Assess your actual capability
   - What are facts? Focus on facts, not fears
   - Do not focus on worst case: Worst case may not be real
   - Do not ignore facts: Facts help control panic

4. USE CONTROL TECHNIQUES:
   - Breathing control: Slow, deep breathing
   - Muscle relaxation: Tense and relax muscles
   - Mental focus: Focus on specific task
   - Grounding: Focus on physical sensations
   - Self-talk: Calm, rational self-talk
   - Do not use techniques that do not work: Find what works for you
   - Do not give up: Keep trying techniques

5. RESTORE THINKING:
   - Clear mind: Clear racing thoughts
   - Focus on present: Focus on current moment
   - Think logically: Use logical thinking
   - Assess options: Identify available options
   - Plan action: Plan what to do
   - Do not rush thinking: Rushed thinking causes mistakes
   - Do not ignore thinking: Clear thinking is essential

6. MAKE DECISIONS:
   - Use clear thinking: Make decisions with clear mind
   - Assess options: Evaluate available options
   - Choose best option: Select best available option
   - Act on decision: Take action based on decision
   - Do not make decisions while panicking: Wait until calm
   - Do not ignore decisions: Decisions are necessary

7. PREVENT RECURRENCE:
   - Identify triggers: What caused panic?
   - Avoid triggers: Avoid panic triggers when possible
   - Prepare for triggers: Prepare response to triggers
   - Practice control: Practice panic control techniques
   - Maintain routine: Routine prevents panic
   - Do not ignore triggers: Triggers cause panic
   - Do not ignore prevention: Prevention prevents panic

8. HELP OTHERS:
   - Recognize others' panic: Identify if others are panicking
   - Calm others: Help others calm down
   - Guide breathing: Help others with breathing
   - Provide reassurance: Reassure others
   - Do not ignore others: Others' panic affects group
   - Do not panic with others: Your calm helps others

FOR SPECIFIC SITUATIONS:

- Assess threat: What is actual threat?
- Assess response: What can you do?
- Use control: Apply panic control
- Take action: Act on threat if possible
- Do not freeze: Freezing does not help
- Do not act recklessly: Reckless action causes problems

PANIC FROM UNCERTAINTY:
- Assess uncertainty: What is unknown?
- Accept uncertainty: Accept what cannot be known
- Focus on known: Focus on what is known
- Plan for uncertainty: Plan for various possibilities
- Do not focus on unknown: Unknown causes panic
- Do not ignore known: Known helps control panic

PANIC FROM OVERWHELM:
- Break down: Break situation into smaller parts
- Prioritize: Focus on most important first
- Take one step: Focus on one step at a time
- Do not focus on everything: Everything is overwhelming
- Do not ignore steps: Steps make situation manageable
