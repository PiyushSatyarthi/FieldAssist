IMMEDIATE DANGERS:
- Getting lost: Disorientation can cause you to become completely lost
- Wrong direction: Losing direction sends you wrong way
- Resource waste: Wandering wastes time and resources
- Exhaustion: Wandering causes unnecessary travel and exhaustion
- Environmental exposure: Being lost increases exposure risk
- Panic: Being lost causes stress and poor decisions
- Separation: Group members can become separated
- Injury: Wandering can lead to dangerous terrain and injury
- Time waste: Being lost wastes critical time
- Reduced survival capability: Being lost reduces all survival capabilities
