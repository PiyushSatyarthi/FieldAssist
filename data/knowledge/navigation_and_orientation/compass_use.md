STEP-BY-STEP ACTIONS:

1. VERIFY COMPASS FUNCTION (DO FIRST):
   - Check compass: Does needle move freely?
   - Check for damage: Is compass damaged?
   - Check for bubbles: Liquid-filled compass should not have bubbles
   - Test movement: Does needle point north?
   - Check readability: Can you read compass clearly?
   - If compass is broken: Do not use, find alternative navigation
   - If compass is damaged: Use with caution, verify with other methods
   - Do not use broken compass: Broken compass causes errors

2. UNDERSTAND MAGNETIC DECLINATION:
   - Find declination: What is declination in your area?
   - Understand declination: Difference between magnetic and true north
   - Understand direction: Is declination east or west?
   - Understand magnitude: How many degrees is declination?
   - Adjust for declination: Add or subtract declination as needed
   - Do not ignore declination: Declination causes significant errors
   - Do not assume declination: Always check declination for area
   - Do not use wrong declination: Wrong declination causes errors

3. ORIENT COMPASS:
   - Hold compass level: Compass must be level to work accurately
   - Point direction arrow: Point arrow toward destination
   - Rotate bezel: Rotate bezel until needle aligns with north mark
   - Read bearing: Read bearing from bezel
   - Do not tilt compass: Tilted compass is inaccurate
   - Do not rush: Rushed orientation causes errors
   - Do not ignore level: Level compass is essential

4. TAKE BEARING TO DESTINATION:
   - Identify destination: Where are you going?
   - Point compass at destination: Point direction arrow at destination
   - Rotate bezel: Rotate bezel until needle aligns with north mark
   - Read bearing: Read bearing from bezel
   - Account for declination: Adjust bearing for declination
   - Record bearing: Remember or write down bearing
   - Do not forget declination: Declination must be accounted for
   - Do not rush: Rushed bearings are inaccurate

5. FOLLOW BEARING:
   - Hold compass level: Compass must be level
   - Align needle: Align needle with north mark
   - Point direction arrow: Point arrow in direction of travel
   - Follow direction: Travel in direction arrow points
   - Check frequently: Verify you are on course regularly
   - Do not deviate: Stay on bearing
   - Do not ignore compass: Continuously refer to compass
   - Do not assume direction: Verify with compass

6. ACCOUNT FOR MAGNETIC INTERFERENCE:
   - Keep compass away from metal: Metal affects compass
   - Keep compass away from electronics: Electronics affect compass
   - Keep compass away from magnets: Magnets affect compass
   - Check for interference: Verify compass away from interference
   - Do not use near metal objects: Metal causes errors
   - Do not use near electronics: Electronics cause errors
   - Do not ignore interference: Interference causes significant errors

7. USE COMPASS WITH MAP:
   - Orient map: Align map with terrain
   - Place compass on map: Place compass on map
   - Align compass with map: Align compass edge with route
   - Rotate map and compass: Rotate until needle points north
   - Read bearing: Read bearing from compass
   - Account for declination: Adjust for declination
   - Do not ignore map: Map provides context
   - Do not ignore declination: Declination must be accounted for

8. VERIFY BEARING:
   - Check bearing regularly: Verify you are on course
   - Use landmarks: Landmarks help verify bearing
   - Use terrain: Terrain helps verify bearing
   - Use multiple methods: Combine compass with other methods
   - Do not assume bearing: Verify regularly
   - Do not ignore discrepancies: If bearing seems wrong, reassess
   - Do not continue if uncertain: Stop and reassess if uncertain
